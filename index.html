Common questions

How do I exit?

Withdrawals are a smart-contract right: redeem() works at any time at the day's NAV, even while new deposits are paused. Larger exits from the staked position can take up to ~7 days (Ethena's unstaking cooldown); a liquidity buffer covers normal redemptions instantly.

What do I pay?

10% of profits above your personal high-water mark plus 1%/yr management, both hard-capped in the contract. You are never charged on your principal, and never twice on the same gain.

Who holds my money?

You do. Funds sit in a non-custodial vault: the operator's keeper can rebalance between whitelisted venues but has no code path to move funds to any third address. Changes to fees, venues and the referral share pass a 48-hour on-chain timelock, so you can exit before they apply; swapping the keeper and pausing are immediate, so a problem can be contained.

Who runs this?

Felice Digennaro, sole operator. Be clear on what the numbers are: the 1007-day curve is a backtest replayed on real market data, not 1007 days of live trading. The system that produces it started publishing forward on 17 Jul 2026, and from that day every run is committed to a public git ledger whose timestamps cannot be rewritten. You are early, and you can watch from day one.

What happens if the operator disappears?

Your withdrawal right does not depend on the operator: redeem() is permissionless and always open. The worst case is unwinding at the venues' pace — never losing access to your funds.

Where does the return actually come from?

Perpetual-futures traders pay a recurring funding fee to the other side of their trades. The fund holds staked ETH and shorts an equal amount of ETH perpetuals — price exposure cancels out, and the fund collects that funding plus staking yield, with leverage capped at 3x by the contract. Full mechanics in the whitepaper.

What if funding stays low for a long time?

Then the fund earns less — the return follows the funding, it is not a fixed rate. When the carry regime weakens the crisis guard moves the book into the safe lending sleeve (~5%/yr) instead of forcing risk, and our design commitment is to waive the management fee while parked, so a lean stretch costs you no more than holding that lending position yourself. You keep the upside of being positioned when the carry regime returns — as it has every cycle since 2019 — without paying for active management that isn't running. (The waiver ships with the next contract revision; see the whitepaper.)

Can I receive the yield monthly instead of compounding it?

Yes — the timing is entirely yours. Your yield lives in your share price: leave it to compound, or withdraw your gain whenever you want. Redemption is always open and permissionless — only you trigger it, with no lock and no schedule. The Monthly income toggle above is a view: it illustrates what taking your gain each month would have looked like across the backtest, applying the high-water-mark rule so a losing month draws nothing. In practice you withdraw when you choose — there is no separate class to pick and nothing is credited automatically.
